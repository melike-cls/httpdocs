What is Calculus (Tartar)?

Foods and beverages consumed throughout the day leave residues on the tooth surface that turn into dental plaque over time. Dental plaque consisting of harmful bacteria adheres to the tooth surface approximately 12 hours after tooth brushing. When regular brushing is not done and the teeth are not brushed correctly and effectively, the dental plaque on the tooth surface hardens with the effect of the minerals in our saliva and tartar (tartar) is formed. We should brush at least 2 times a day to protect our teeth from dental plaque and tartar formation.

Tartar, which is similar to lime, adversely affects the health of the teeth and gums of the individual. Tartar can lead to gingivitis and gingival recession problems and even tooth loss when it progresses. When gingival recession begins, tartar fills the place of the receding gingiva. It has been observed that some of the patients who are faced with such a situation do not realize their gum disease or realize it quite late.

In order to prevent the negative effects that may occur due to calculus, regular dental check-ups and calculus cleaning should be performed.

What Causes Tartar?

The main causes of tartar formation in the mouth of individuals:

  • Not having a habit of brushing teeth or not brushing their teeth correctly,
  • Saliva structure,
  • Food residues remaining between the teeth as a result of not using dental floss or using dental floss correctly,
  • Not using mouthwash,
  • Unilateral chewing habit,
  • Improper dental fillings,
  • It is carelessness about oral and dental health

How to Prevent Tartar Formation?

What can be done to prevent the formation of dental calculus?

  • Teeth should be brushed regularly and correctly,
  • Toothpastes containing fluoride should be used,
  • Dental floss should be used
  • Mouthwash should be applied,
  • Care should be taken not to consume foods that may adversely affect dental health.

After your tartar cleaning, you can get care recommendations from your dentist.

How Is Teeth Calculus (Tartar) Performed? What are the Stages of Detertrage?

Tartar cleaning should be done by a dentist every 6 months at all ages. If there are tartars that damage the teeth and gums, it is necessary to have calculus cleaning, which is often painless and non-bleeding, called detertraj (scaling cleaning) for oral and dental health.

The sequence of tooth tartar removal process:

  • First, the dentist performs an intraoral examination.
  • The dentist decides whether standard dental cleaning or advanced dental cleaning is required.
  • Using ultrasonic devices and some tools, it cleans the plaque and tartar on the gum line and between your teeth without damaging the gum tissue,
  • If the tartar has progressed towards the root of the tooth, curettage is performed,
  • The ultrasonic cleaner heads used give micro vibrations to the calculus part and ensure the disintegration of the tartar. Then, polishing is done with a mechanical brush and stains on the teeth are removed with this process.
  • Anesthesia is rarely used for tartar removal treatment,
  • After scaling, it polishes to prevent the formation of new calculus and to brighten the teeth.
  • The dentist gives information to the patients about how oral hygiene should be done after scaling. “How to brush teeth correctly and how to use dental floss?” The answers to the questions are explained.
  • In addition to all these, the root surfaces of some teeth should also be cleaned during tartar cleaning. In this case, the dentist performs curettage. It removes the tartar carefully and in a controlled manner and cleans the related area. In this way, a protective procedure is performed against gum diseases.

What is Detertrage, Curettage and Polishing?

In tartar cleaning, factors such as the presence of gum disease in the patient and the size of the depth of the tartar play an active role in determining the method to be used. These methods are:

Detertrage

It is the process of cleaning the tartar on the teeth with ultrasonic devices by the dentist. In this frequently preferred method, the patient should not have gum disease.

Curettage

It is a safe and common method preferred for tartar cleaning in cases of gum disease. To this method; It is needed in patients who have not done dental scaling, general dental health and oral hygiene properly for a long time. It is a method used in cases where there are very dense and deep dental stones.

Tartar progresses and is widely found on the surface of the tooth root, teeth and under the gums and causes gingivitis. If left untreated, this condition causes loosening of the teeth and tooth loss. While the use of antibiotics is often sufficient to relieve inflammation in other inflammatory formations in the body, antibiotics may not be sufficient in the treatment of gingivitis. The process in which tartar and inflammation accumulated on the teeth, under the gingiva, on the root surface are cleaned is called deep cleaning. This method is also called curettage.

How Is Curettage Dental Treatment Performed?

  • The patient's mild calculus is cleaned.
  • Local anesthesia is applied to the patient.
  • Small incisions are made on the patient's gums, the gum is separated from the root, inflammation and tartar under the gums are cleaned.
  • When the deep cleaning process is finished, the gums are sutured and the gums are attached to the tooth again.

    • What Should Be Considered After Curettage?

    • No smoking for 48 hours,
    • Alcohol should not be consumed,
    • Hot, cold or heavily spiced foods should not be eaten,
    • For oral and dental health, the face should not be hit.

    What is Polishing - How is it done?

    The process of polishing the teeth after the tartar is completely cleaned is called polishing. The dentist passes the chewing surface of the tooth one by one with the help of a special brush for 15-20 minutes. It prevents the accumulation of tartar and bacteria on the surface of the tooth chewer. With the dental polishing process, the formation of caries on the teeth is prevented. It can be easily applied for sensitive teeth..

    What should be considered after dental scaling? Calculus

    • The gap formed after scaling may cause the patient's tongue to get stuck in the area that often feels lumpy in the first few days.
    • If calculus is cleaned in the later period of gum disease, when calculus is intense, the teeth may shake a little.
    • Consumption of hot - cold food may cause sensitivity in the first few days. These sensitivities are normal.
    • Tobacco products, tea, coffee, foods containing food coloring, and acidic beverages that cause discoloration of the teeth should not be consumed in the first days after the cleaning process.

    Benefits of Tartar Removal

    Teeth cleaning is a procedure that should be done regularly by everyone who cares about their oral and dental health. When the tartars, which will cause serious dental problems from gingival recession to inflammation, and even cause tooth loss, are cleaned, your teeth will return to their healthy structure.

    Benefits of tartar removal treatment:

    • Prevents gingival diseases, gingivitis, gingival recession and tooth decay.
    • It prevents bone inflammations.
    • Your dental health is protected.
    • Bad breath due to dental calculus, gum bleeding is prevented.
    • Possible tooth loss is prevented.
    • Patients feel freshness in the mouth with the cavity formed after dental scaling.
    • Since the patient does not feel pain during the procedure, he is not afraid of having a dental scaling procedure.

    Does Teeth Cleaning (Detertrage) Damage Teeth? Is Cleaning Teeth Harmful?

    The rumor that the calculus cleaning (detertrage) process is dangerous is not based on any scientific evidence. The main danger is the situations that may arise as a result of not cleaning the tartar.

    In teeth that have not been bleached for many years; gingival recession, gingival diseases, tooth loss may occur. As a result of tooth loss, there is a risk of bone loss in the jawbone. In this case, graft bone powder should be applied in another dental treatment that should be done to a patient. All these processes are more tiring and more painful than tartar cleaning.

    Taking care of oral hygiene in order to prevent tartar formation, regular dental check-ups, and dental scaling procedures are the easiest ways to protect oral and dental health.

    Tartar cleaning, which is done carefully by expert dentists using the necessary materials, does not harm the tooth enamel and teeth. Cleaning calculus is not harmful.

    How Many Hours Does Curettage Take?

    The curettage process, in which the deepened tartar is cleaned from the tooth roots, and the necrotic tissues on the tooth root are also cleaned, takes 40-60 minutes.

    Is Tartar Harmful?

    Tartar can cause discoloration of the teeth, gum infection, gum recession, tooth loss and even melting of the jawbone. Tartar is harmful to oral and dental health
