Teeth Whitening-Bleaching

What is Teeth Whitening?

Tooth whitening (Bleaching) is the process of whitening the yellowed and stained teeth for various reasons in a natural tooth color or, if desired, lighter and whiter than the natural tooth color. Bleaching process is approved by the World Health Organization, teeth whitening gels that only dentists can use; It starts with the application of tooth enamel by dentists in a clinical setting. Then, the applied tooth whitening gel is activated by heating with a laser. A process called laser teeth whitening is applied. Some whitening gels are also activated without the need for laser application. Your doctor will choose the gel to suit you. The process is repeated in several sets and finally the gel is cleaned. After the gel is cleaned, the teeth whitening process is completed. If smiling with white teeth is your dream, we recommend you to come to our clinic for bleaching for this beauty that you can reach in one session.

Why is Teeth Whitening done?

Over time, due to the side effects of the drugs used, insufficient attention to oral and dental health, and food consumption habits, tooth enamel (the top layer of the tooth) may change color. This yellowing is irritating. For dental aesthetics, bleaching (teeth whitening) can be preferred for a cleaner, healthier and more beautiful appearance. We can say that whitening the teeth is the most aesthetically innocent and pleasant intervention made to the human body in a clinical environment by a specialist dentist at the patient's own request.

What are the Teeth Whitening methods in Turkey?

Teeth whitening methods are of three types: Office bleaching, Home bleaching, and Internal Bleaching. These three procedures can be applied separately as well as with the patient's request and the dentist's approval.

    Home Bleaching

    When it comes to teeth whitening at home, it may come to mind to do teeth whitening at home. Home bleaching is not done with the products you buy and use on your own. Dentists do not approve of the purchase and use of such whitening products without the advice of a physician. For home teeth whitening, your dentist must first recommend it. Afterwards, it is a procedure applied at home by applying a tooth whitening gel (which should be used only with the recommendation of the dentist) into the transparent plaque that will be placed on your teeth prepared by your dentist. For home teeth whitening, transparent dental plaque should be used for 4-6 hours daily and the treatment should be continued for at least 15 days. Before considering home teeth whitening as the first choice, attention should be paid to the recommendations of our dentists to preserve teeth whitening. While following the recommendations below, you can come to our clinic for more aesthetic smiles and whiter teeth and receive the advice of our physicians and the treatments they deem appropriate. To preserve the natural whiteness of your teeth:

    • Do not consume excessively beverages such as tea, coffee and cola that may cause discoloration of the teeth and foods containing food coloring
    • Stop smoking,
    • Brush your teeth for 2 minutes at least 2 times a day,
    • Use dental floss,
    • Go to regular dentist checks and have your teeth that need treatment treated without delay,

    Also, never follow any teeth whitening recipe you hear or see online without your dentist's approval. Commercially available tooth whitening gels and special tooth whitening pastes can damage your teeth and gums. In order not to cause an irreversible accident, it is the right thing to take action with the approval of your dentist. The most appropriate procedures to be done at home are our routine oral and dental health care.

    Office Bleaching

    It is the right choice for teeth whitening. In this bleaching method, your dentist performs the procedure in the clinic in 2-3 sets in a single session. Tooth color is opened several tones in a single session. Bleaching is a patient satisfaction procedure that has been successfully applied in our clinic for years

    Internal Bleaching

    Internal Bleaching is the process of whitening teeth that have had root canal treatment from the inside out. First, the pulp of the tooth undergoing root canal treatment is removed. A special substance is placed inside the tooth. This substance dissolves different discolorations on the teeth. In this way, teeth whiten.

What is done before Teeth Whitening?

The patient has a preliminary examination by our dentist. If there are dental treatments necessary for oral and dental health, they are performed. These:

  • Our dentist treats gingivitis or gum health.
  • Tooth calculus is cleaned.
  • If you have rotten or broken teeth, aesthetic filling is done.
  • If you have deep caries, Endodontic treatment (canal treatment) is performed.
  • Orthodontic (problematic jaw structure, too frequent and overlapping teeth) problems are eliminated.
  • It is checked whether the cause of your yellow teeth is a genetic deficiency.

Your dentist will take care of your oral and dental health and create the necessary infrastructure for the whitening process.

How is Bleaching done? How Is Teeth Whitening made?

  • Necessary dental treatments are performed before whitening.
  • Photographs of the teeth are taken before the procedure. In this way, although the teeth that underwent bleaching will reach their full color after 24 hours, the patient will see the first color difference before they come out of the clinker.
  • A reagent is placed in the patient's mouth.
  • Protective is applied to the gums.
  • Dense-consistent tooth whitening gels are applied.
  • Tooth whitening is done with laser.
  • Available in non-irradiated gels. Your doctor may also prefer these gels.
  • The process is repeated enough.
  • The tooth surface is cleaned and the process is completed.
  • The initial whiteness of the tooth color and the whiteness of the teeth before the procedure are compared. Patient satisfaction begins at this stage.

While the process takes about 1 hour, it takes 24 hours for the tooth color to get the full tone.

What should be considered after Teeth Whitening?

Points to be considered after the whitening process applied to restore the old appearance of teeth that have lost their whiteness for various reasons:

  • The first 24-36 hours after the whitening process is important. In order to avoid sensitization, care should be taken that the foods and beverages we consume are not too hot or too cold,
  • The difference in air temperature can also cause sensitivity when passing from one middle to another. To prevent this, a mask should be used,
  • Avoid foods that can change the color of the teeth (tea, coffee, cigarettes, cola, wine, etc.),
  • Consumption of harmful substances such as cigarettes, which deeply affect our health, should be completely stopped,

Oral and dental health care, which is in our daily routine, should be done carefully without neglecting it

Why do Teeth turn yellow?

The whiteness of the teeth can be reduced in various ways. These:

  • Structural disorder that occurs during the growth and development period of children,
  • Medicines used while in the womb or antibiotics used as a baby,
  • Mistakes (cigarettes, etc.) and excesses (tea, coffee…) in the products we consume daily,
  • Old dental operations,
  • Tooth decay,
  • Advanced age,
  • Improper oral and dental care,
  • It is the use of drugs for a long time.

Why do Teeth turn yellow?

  • Those who are visually disturbed by their tooth color,
  • Individuals who want their teeth to look even more beautiful,
  • People who have completed their jaw development (Generally, the development of the jawbone of individuals over the age of 16 is completed. However, the development of the jaw may differ from person to person. It will be best to be directed by your dentist for this.)

Anyone who wishes can have teeth whitening done by our experienced specialist dentists by making an appointment with our clinic.

Why do teeth turn yellow?

  • Those who are 15 years old or younger and those whose jaw development has not yet been completed should not have bleaching,
  • If the patient's oral and dental health is not suitable, teeth whitening should not be performed before the oral and dental health treatments are completed,
  • Those with orthodontic problems should not have a teeth whitening procedure without the approval of the specialist dentist,
  • Teeth whitening should not be performed on teeth that are yellow due to genetic

Due to the genetic structure, enamel may not have formed in the permanent teeth of some people after their childhood milk teeth fall out. In these teeth, the teeth appear yellow because there is no tooth enamel that gives shine and aesthetics to the teeth. In such cases, the recommendation of dentists is to have one of the types of laminated veneer, zirconium veneer, emax empress veneer. In the procedures to be performed on the teeth that are yellow due to the genetic structure, the procedure should be performed by taking into account the age and jaw development of the patient..

Frequently Asked Questions about Tooth Whitening

Teeth Whitening cost, prices, how much is Teeth Whitening fee?

The factors affecting the teeth whitening prices are the oral and dental health of the patient for whom bleaching will be performed, the pre-treatments required to start the teeth whitening process, the experience of the dentist, and the location of the clinic. For detailed information, we recommend that you make an appointment with our clinic and be examined by our physicians.

How long does the Teeth Whitening process take?

Dentists complete the teeth whitening process in an average of 45-60 minutes in the clinic. 24-36 hours after the procedure, your tooth color takes its final form. The duration of the whitening process varies depending on the tooth structure and consumption habits of the person.

How long is the Permanence of the Teeth Whitening process?

The permanence of the teeth whitening process is 4 years on average if the teeth are taken care of and the food and beverages with dyestuffs are consumed carefully. If oral and dental health care routines are not neglected during this period, the effect of the procedure will be much longer.
