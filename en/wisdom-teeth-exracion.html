What is Tooth Extraction?

Tooth extraction is the process of removing teeth that have suffered significant damage, decay, infection or crooked teeth, especially wisdom teeth. Tooth extraction, as a standard procedure, can remove bacteria from the problem tooth and treat your overall oral health. Tooth extraction is performed by a dentist or oral surgeon with local, general, and intravenous anesthesia or a combination of these.

Why would a tooth need to be extracted? In which situations is Tooth Extractıon applıed? When is Tooth Removal recommended?

Dentists prefer to keep the teeth in the mouth if other treatment techniques are sufficient, rather than pulling the teeth as long as possible. If the tooth is beyond repair, tooth extraction may be inevitable. Dentists may recommend tooth extraction in some of the following situations:

  • If the tooth decay is very heavy.
  • If the decay or fracture of the tooth cannot be treated with a filling, root canal treatment and other similar treatment methods.
  • If there are crooked or many teeth, they prevent other teeth from coming out.
  • If there is a broken tooth in the mouth.
  • If there is an impacted tooth.
  • If the impacted wisdom tooth causes pain and damages the adjacent teeth.
  • If the jawbone is damaged due to severe gum disease and tissues are lost.
  • If an infection in the disturbed tooth poses a risk in patients undergoing chemotherapy treatment and organ transplant.
  • If the appearance of the tooth is an aesthetic problem.
  • If the teeth cannot perform their function due to shaking.
  • If the tooth is displaced from its normal axis (luxation), other tooth injuries have occurred.
  • If there are tumors or non-removable cysts in the tooth's root.
  • Tooth extraction can be done as a last resort if the listed conditions have occurred.
  • The dentist will make the best decision about tooth extraction for the patient.

What should be done before Tooth Removal?

Before planning what will be done in all kinds of tooth extraction, including wisdom tooth extraction, first of all, the tooth will be x-rayed for evaluation.

  • Before tooth extraction, mouth and teeth should be cleaned.
  • Your dentist should be informed about the drugs, vitamins, supplements and over-the-counter drugs. Shortly, you will receive an intravenous drug treatment called bisphosphonate for a different disease, this should be shared with the dentist, and tooth extraction should be done before drug treatment. Otherwise, bone death may occur in the jaw.
  • Your dentist should be informed if there is congenital heart disease, diabetes, liver disease, thyroid disease, kidney disease, high blood pressure, artificial joint, heart valve disorders, an adrenal disease caused by insufficiency of certain hormones, immune system problem, bacterial disease existing in the inner membrane of the heart.
  • If the surgery will take a long time, if there is an infection, if the immune system is weak, if a specific medical condition is known, they may administer antibiotic treatment before tooth extraction.
  • If you want the tooth extraction process to be successful if you are going to have intravenous (IV) anesthesia, wear loose and short-sleeved clothing, do not eat or drink anything 6-8 hours in advance, do not smoke, postpone the appointment by telling the dentist if there is a cold, Notifying the dentist if there is nausea or vomiting that will require anesthesia, and having a companion with you if general anesthesia is to be applied are the conditions that should be considered.

How is Tooth Extraction performed in Istanbul? How is the tooth extracted?

Tooth extraction is done in two different ways, depending on whether your tooth is visible in the outer region or if it is a buried tooth.

  • Simple Subtraction

    • The area to be extracted is anesthetized with local anesthesia so that no pain is felt during the extraction. The only pressure is felt. The dentist then moves the tooth left, and right with an instrument called an elevator to loosen the tooth and uses forceps to remove the tooth.

  • Surgical Shooting

    • In severe cases such as impacted wisdom tooth extraction and similar cases, the patient is given both local anesthesia and intravenous anesthesia. If the dentist sees a medical condition, he may also apply general anesthesia. The general dentist or oral surgeon cuts the gum in a small incision. After providing a large area to see the tooth to be operated under the gum, extraction can be performed. If necessary, the bone around the tooth can be removed before extraction. The gum is stitched. Whether it is a wisdom tooth extraction, impacted tooth or visible tooth extraction, whether tooth extraction will be difficult depends on the condition of the roots and the connection between the tooth and the bone. While dislocated, loose teeth are easily pulled out, tooth extraction is difficult in case of uneven tooth root structure, impacted tooth, and severe caries, and the tooth that does not come out in one piece is removed in pieces.

What should be done after Tooth Extraction?

  • After tooth extraction, the dentist places cotton or gauze on the operated area. The tampon, which will stop the bleeding and contribute to the formation of a blood clot for a healthy recovery, should be bitten by the patient for 20-25 minutes. The tampon should be kept in the mouth without changing it frequently. The blood clot will form a natural buffer in the space where the tooth was extracted and will prepare for the bone structure to be filled in the future. If the blood clot is not taken care of, it cannot occur, and thus the wound heals late, and a painful healing period is experienced.
  • If pain is felt after tooth extraction, painkillers recommended by the dentist can be taken. However, aspirin should not be used as it has a blood thinning effect. Because if the blood is diluted, there will be no coagulation. Pain and pain may occur at the places of the needles used to give anesthesia before tooth extraction. These aches and pains will pass in an average of 2 days.
  • Light bleeding, resembling leakage, which can last up to 24 hours after a tooth extraction, is expected. In this case, if you panic, the job will become even more difficult. A clean, sterile cotton pad can be used if the bleeding is heavy. The tampon should remain in the area for up to 30 minutes. If the bleeding cannot be stopped in this way, a dentist should be consulted immediately. If the bleeding has become continuous, the buffer period should be extended and not be changed frequently. In this process, rinsing the mouth increases bleeding.
  • Smoking and alcohol should not be used for at least 24 hours after tooth extraction. Smoking and alcohol prevent the formation of blood clots.
  • Extremely hot and cold foods and drinks should not be consumed after tooth extraction. This can start the bleeding again. It is recommended to consume a few warm and soft things.
  • If the tooth extraction took a long time and were difficult, it would be good to apply cold at intervals of 5-10 minutes every hour for 8 hours to control swelling and bruising.

Does a Tooth Extraction hurt? Is Tooth Extraction a painful procedure in Turkey?

Many patients think that they will feel pain during tooth extraction. However, since the patients are given local anesthesia during the procedure, patients don’t feel pain. The dentist checks whether the area is numb before starting the tooth extraction process and starts the tooth extraction process accordingly. In some patients, mild pain and aches lasting 2-3 days may occur depending on the pain threshold after tooth extraction. Even if the tooth is inflamed or the tooth extraction process is complex, the pain may continue long after extraction. In these cases, dentist-recommended pain relievers can be used.

Frequently Asked Questions about Wisdom Tooth Removal

How long after an extraction does it take for alveolitis to occur?

After tooth extraction, a blood clot should form at the extraction site. If the clot does not form, inflammation and pain occur. This is called alveolitis. Alveolitis is one of the most common complications after tooth extraction. Alveolitis; It is a condition that varies according to the place where it occurs, causes pain, weakness, fatigue and discomfort that spreads to the forehead and around the eyes, occurs approximately 1-3 days after the surgical application, the form of clot formation is partially or wholly distorted and manifests itself with unwanted lousy breath. Alveolitis pain usually does not go away with analgesic drugs and can last for 7-10 days on average. Poor oral and dental care, oral bacteria, advanced gum problems, traumatic tooth extraction, root and bone fragments left in the extraction area, damage to clot formation, pregnancy control drugs, smoking and alcohol consumption can cause alveolitis development.

How long does a Tooth Extraction take in Turkey?

Since people who will have tooth extraction are worried before the procedure, they wonder about the answers to the questions such as how many minutes the tooth extraction takes or how long it takes to heal. The duration of a tooth extraction varies depending on factors such as the connection of the tooth with the bone, the number of roots in the tooth to be extracted and whether there is a change in the direction of the roots, the position of the tooth with other teeth, and the size of the tooth if there is a fracture. In complicated cases, the procedure may take longer. Although the exact time cannot be said for the reasons we have explained for tooth extraction, in general, a problem-free tooth can be extracted in 15-20 minutes.

Can I smoke after a Tooth Extraction?

Smoking and alcohol use prevent the formation of blood clots, which are necessary for a healthy recovery. Smoking can also cause infection. Smoking delays healing after tooth extraction and makes our lives more problematic as it harms the immune system. After tooth extraction, smoking should be stopped at least until the wounds in the extraction area heal.
