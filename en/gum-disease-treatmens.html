Gingival Inflammation- Gingival Diseases-Gum Disease Treatment

For teeth with a healthy and aesthetic appearance, it is not enough to have healthy teeth. In addition, teeth eat and enjoy aesthetics for it; The jawbone (alveoli), the periodontal fibers affecting the jawbone and the gingiva must also be healthy. That's what's happening to your son's dental experiences as well as your gum experiences. One of the most common and severe gum problems is gingivitis. Gingivitis is a gum disease that causes infection in the gums, is highly contagious, can cause other diseases and tooth loss if not treated, and is caused by many factors, including inadequate oral and dental care. If left untreated, it can lead to tooth loss. Therefore, it would not be wrong to say that the way to have healthy teeth is through healthy gums.

What is Gum Inflammation?

Gingivitis is an inflammatory disease in the gums due to unbalanced and inadequate nutrition, not paying attention to personal oral and dental care, and some diseases. The most important cause of this disease, which develops as a bacterial infection, is the lack of regular oral and dental care. In cases where necessary oral and dental care is not provided, In the form of a colorless, sticky and thin film layer, a structure called bacterial plaque is formed, which is formed by pathogenic bacteria on the teeth and gums. In addition to dental caries, this structure is the leading cause of gum diseases. As a result of the bacteria in this bacterial plaque producing acidic and toxic substances, conditions such as gingival swelling, redness and bleeding in the lower part of the tooth are called gingivitis. Gingivitis needs to be treated immediately without neglect. Otherwise, in the later stages, it will cause a more serious dental disease, gingival recession (periodontitis) and subsequent tooth loss problems. Therefore, gingivitis is not a disease to be neglected.

What are the symptoms of Gum Inflammation?

A healthy should be gum-colored pink, dull, stiff and tight. As a result of gingivitis, this structure deteriorates, and the following conditions occur.

  • Bad breath and bad taste in the mouth
  • Gum bleeding that occurs spontaneously or when something hard is eaten
  • Bleeding of teeth while brushing and flossing
  • Redness of the gums
  • Receding gums
  • The formation of cold and hot sensitivity in the gingival
  • Swelling and swelling of the gums
  • Pain and ache during speaking and chewing
  • Discoloration of teeth in the form of red-purple
  • Inflammations on the reddened gums or inside the palate and lip
  • Shaking, falling and spacing of teeth

If gingivitis is not treated after the appearance of these symptoms, it can affect all tissues and cause more severe tooth and tissue loss.

  1. Oral and Dental care

    2. The main and most common cause of gingivitis is insufficient oral and dental care. Bacteria in the mouth combine with food and form a colorless, thin, sticky bacterial plaque on the surface of the teeth and gums. If this bacterial plaque is not removed by oral and dental care practices such as daily tooth brushing and flossing, it will lead to gingivitis over time. In the later stages, if this bacterial plaque is not cleaned, it starts to harden on the gum line and calculus (tartar) forms. As the bacterial plaque and tartar stay on the teeth and gums longer, the gums are damaged, and inflammation occurs due to the effect of bacteria. Gums become red and swollen and begin to bleed. If this condition is left untreated, it causes gum recession and tooth loss.

What are the types of Gum Diseases?

Gum diseases are health problems that are often unnoticed or taken very seriously. Bleeding in the gums, spontaneously or due to the effect of a hard substance, is the first and most important symptom of these diseases. Gum diseases are divided into two parts gingivitis and periodontitis. Gingivitis is a gum disease that occurs before periodontitis. However, not all gingivitis turns into periodontitis. Most people get gingivitis at some point in their lives and ignore it because of its mild symptoms. However, if gingivitis is not treated promptly, it can cause more significant problems. However, you can prevent this disease from occurring by having personal oral care practices (tooth brushing, flossing, gargling) and routine dental examinations.

    Gingivitis

    It is an inflammatory gum disease that occurs when personal oral care is not performed adequately, is caused by bacterial plaque and is limited only to the gums. When personal oral care such as tooth brushing, mouthwash and flossing is not performed, a colorless, sticky and thin layer of bacterial plaque forms around the teeth. The bacteria in the bacterial plaque secrete acid and toxic substances that cause damage to the teeth and gums. This bacterial plaque, which is not removed from the surface of the teeth daily with necessary oral care practices, begins to turn into calculus along the gum line after 72 hours. This accumulation over time causes inflammation of the gums. Gum swelling, redness and bleeding are observed. No damage has occurred yet in the teeth, jawbone and connective tissue at this stage. This gum disease, which is limited only to the gums, is called gingivitis. The dentist's dental scaling procedure can eliminate this dental disease caused by bacterial plaque. In addition, gingivitis is treated in a short time, and the gums are restored to a healthy structure if the patient performs personal oral care regularly and correctly.

    Periodontitis

    When gingivitis disease is not treated, it is an advanced gum disease that occurs due to the spread of the infection that starts in the gums to the tooth root, jawbone and the periodontal ligaments that connect them and destroys these tissues. If gingivitis develops towards the bone, the teeth and gums separate from each other, and periodontal spaces form between the teeth and gums. In these cavities, plaque and tartar accumulate more, and inflammation progresses. If periodontitis has reached advanced levels and has become uncomfortable for the patient, it means that sometimes irreversible tissue damage has occurred. Now teeth wobble, and tooth loss occurs. One of the most important causes of tooth loss in adults is gum disease. Therefore, bleeding gums in the mouth should be taken into account and a dentist should be consulted about this situation.

How to treat Gum Diseases in Istanbul?

Early diagnosis and early intervention of gum diseases are vital for oral and dental health. Thus, the disease will be eliminated before it reaches much further and irreversible points. In treating gingivitis, the dentist determines a roadmap and treatment according to the patient's condition. The main goal in the treatment of gum disease is to remove the leading cause of gingivitis infection from the gums. The treatment method or methods to be applied are determined by considering the extent of the disease, previous treatments, if any, and the patient's general health status. Whether the disease is in the initial or advanced stages, Many different treatment methods may be required, from non-surgical treatment to surgical treatment. Depending on the patient's condition, antibiotic use and dental scaling treatment are performed if there is an initial infection. Cleaning the tartar and bacterial plaque on the tooth surface removes the bacteria causing the infection from the environment. Thus, the gums are reattached to the teeth, a healthy color structure is achieved, and advanced tissue damage is prevented. Surgical intervention may be required if there is a more advanced level of infection. In this case, surgical interventions such as opening the gums and removing tissue loss are applied.

Frequently Asked Questions about Gum Disease Treatments

How long are Gum Diseases treated?

Gingivitis, one of the gingival diseases in which no damage has occurred yet in the tissues outside the gingiva, can be cured entirely. However, the damaged tissues cannot be completely restored in periodontitis, which spreads to other tissues besides the gingiva. Surgical intervention and restoration of damaged tissues with various drugs can be performed in periodontitis, where there is progressive tissue loss. These treatments are carried out over a more extended period. Generally, gingival diseases can be treated for periods ranging from 1 month to 1 year, depending on the stage of the disease.

What happens if Gingivitis is not treated?

If gum diseases at the beginning level are not treated in a timely manner, it causes damage to teeth, gums, jawbone and connective tissues, leading to gingival recession, loosening of the teeth and tooth loss. There is a recurrence of untreated gingivitis. In scientific studies, it has been reported that bacteria that cause gingivitis can pass into the bloodstream and affect organs such as the lungs and heart.

How to prevent Gum Inflammation in Turkey?

The most important way to prevent gum diseases is to carry out personal oral and dental care daily. In this context;

  • Teeth should be brushed correctly at least twice a day.
  • Dental floss should be used for cleaning between teeth.
  • Antibacterial mouthwashes should be used.

    • Except those;

  • Tooth cleaning at least once a year
  • Routine dental examination checks every six months without interruption procedures are crucial measures in preventing gingivitis and diseases.

Does Gingival Recession heal by itself?

Gingival recession is a severe gum disease that occurs as a result of advanced gingivitis and exposes the roots of the teeth. Therefore, it is out of the question for the gingival recession problem to return to its former state without the necessary gingival treatments. With the application of the necessary gum treatments, the extracted gums return to their old positions.

Which vitamin deficiency causes Gingival Inflammation?

Vitamin C is a substance used in the growth and repair of all body tissues. It is involved in the production of protein necessary for the growth and repair of many tissues, such as skin, connective tissue, tooth repair, and healing of wounds. For this reason, vitamin C deficiency can cause increased gum tissue sensitivity and cause gum diseases to occur more quickly.

Is mouthwash with salt water good for Gingival Inflammation?

Since the number of bacteria in the mouth will be reduced by mouthwash made with salt water, it can be considered a preventive measure against the formation of gingivitis. Gargling with salt water can be applied 2-3 times a week..

Does Gum Inflammation explode? Does Gum Inflammation spread?

The structure formed by pus and bacteria, which causes gingivitis and forms in the gums, is called an abscess among people. The abscess may burst if not treated promptly. This can be understood by the formation of bad taste and odor in the mouth. In case of eruption of the abscess, the infection in the gum can spread to other parts of the body through the blood, causing severe complications. The eruption of the abscess does not mean that the gingivitis will go away independently without any treatment. This is only a temporary situation; if it is not treated, infection in the same area and even previously healthy gingival areas is very high.

Which doctor should I go to for Gum Disease in Turkey? What is Periodontics?

Periodontitis; It is one of the dentistry departments that perform the treatment of gum diseases and surgical interventions on the gums. As soon as gingivitis symptoms appear, a periodontist dentist should be consulted.

In these operations, local anesthesia is used to prevent severe pain. There may be a pain for a while after the operation. Depending on the size of the surgical operation, your pain's severity may differ. However, with the painkillers given to you by your dentist, you can get through this process with the least amount of pain.

How can I make my gums healthier?

Taking preventive measures before gingival diseases occur is more accessible than the complex and troublesome treatment processes. In this context, to protect the gums and keep them in a healthier structure;

  • Brush your teeth at least twice a day with a toothbrush
  • Don't forget to use dental floss while cleaning your teeth.
  • Use fluoride toothpaste
  • Get more calcium and vitamin C
  • Stay away from cigarettes and tobacco products
  • Stay away from stress
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet
